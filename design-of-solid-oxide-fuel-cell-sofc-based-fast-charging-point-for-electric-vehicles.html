2.5 Design of Solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) based Fast Charging Point for Electric Vehicles

Master project

2.5.1 Context

An industrial collaborator would like to design solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) based fast charging points for electric vehicles (EV). In this project, bio-ethanol and/or bio-ethane will be used as fuel to the SOFC system, and prototype of fast charging point will have power output of more than 100 kW. The SOFC has a very high fuel to electricity efficiency, and waste heat available from the system can be used in the operation of fuel cell and/or supply heat to industrial clusters or district heating network.

In this project, master student will use SOFC model developed by GEM (EPFL) / IPESE (EPFL) in Aspen Plus, and will interface it with the OSMOSE platform (developed by IPESE), which is a tool for the design, integration and optimization of energy system. This project will require: (1) optimal design of the SOFC stack to achieve high electric efficiency, (2) upscaling of the SOFC stacks to achieve desired output power for the protype, (3) optimize the operation of fast charging points to satisfy demand profiles.

As the first step, a steady-state process design / model will be modified and optimized. Then, student will compare different system configurations and perform a multi-objective optimization to explore the tradeoff among conflicting objectives, such as energy efficiency, prototype cost, weight and volume of prototype. For each optimal configuration, an optimal heat exchange network will be synthesized that will be used by the industrial collaborator as the starting point for designing heat exchangers, and other components.

2.5.2 The project

This project will be organised in the following steps.

  • Analysis and understanding of the research problem.
  • Modification and optimization of SOFC model.
  • Design and configuration of prototype (fast charging point).
  • Operation optimization of prototype.

2.5.3 Skills

  • Experience with flowsheeting software (e.g., Aspen Plus)
  • Understanding of energy integration and process optimization
  • Results interpretation and report writing
  • Coding skills: Lua, AMPL
  • Lectures: Modelling and Optimisation of Energy systems, Energy Conversion and/or Renewable Energy

2.5.4 Administrative

2.5.5 Location
