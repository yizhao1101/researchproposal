3 Transition to Clean Road Transport with Low Carbon Fuels

Master thesis

Autumn 2022

3.1 Context

Road transport provides broad-scale benefits for society, such as connectivity and territorial cohesion, better access to markets and employment opportunities. However, at the same time, road transport is one of the largest emission sources in Europe, accounting for approximately 18% of total EU-28 GHG emissions (“A Clean Planet for All” 2018). Limiting global warming to 1.5 °C will require rapid decarbonization of the road transport sector, which means reducing emissions by at least 60% by 2050 as compared to the levels in 1990 (“Transport – Facts and Figures” n.d.).

For passenger or light-duty vehicles, electrical vehicles (EVs) have been proven to be a mature technology to replace the conventional fossil fuel based vehicles. The ban of internal combustion engine from 2035 was officially announced by EU in June 2022. However, as the range and mass of the vehicles increase, electrification becomes increasingly difficult. Therefore, alternative low carbon fuels and their respective powertrains are required to achieve deep decarbonization of heavy duty transport.

Low carbon fuels can be based on two different feedstocks: sustainable biomass(biofuels) or renewable electricity (e-fuels). Their corresponding manufacturing processes, fuels and powertrains are included in Figure 3.1. It is worth noting that the same fuels (chemicals) can be produced from various feedstocks and there are competitions between a number of low-carbon drive-technologies. The transition to net zero is critical but never straightforward.

This project aims to obtain an overview of the road transport sector in Europe. The student will build databases of different low carbon fuel manufacturing technologies in Figure 3.1 and identify their cost, environmental impacts, and technology readiness levels. Linear programming method will be applied to generate a series of potential solutions and the optimal one will be selected based multi-criteria analysis.

Figure 3.1: A concept structure of the fuels and powertrains with potential applications for the road transport sector

3.2 Literature Study

An enormous quantity of researches have been conducted and several excellent review articles provides an extensive picture of the decarbonization pathways in EU transport sector. Gray et al. (Gray et al. 2021) did a comprehensive analysis of various low carbon energy carriers in the haulage (heavy vehicle) sector and identified two key barriers facing the update of alternatives to fossil fuels — fuel costs and a lack of refueling infrastructure. Noll et al. (Noll et al. 2022) conducted a total cost of ownership analysis for road-freight in Europe. It was suggested that the increasing policy flexibility and vehicle efficiency were the key enabler to drive the competitiveness of low carbon fuels.

Concawe is the scientific division of the European Refining Association. In 2021, Concawe published a scenario analysis study for the transition towards low carbon fuels by 2050 (“Transition Towards Low Carbon Fuels by 2050: Scenario Analysis for the European Refining Sector” 2021). In this report, three scenarios with different levels of low carbon fuel penetrations in road transport were proposed. In addition, the required technologies and investment to achieve the scenarios were identified and calculated.

3.3 The missions

Low carbon fuel has always been one of the main research interests in the IPESE group. Special focuses have been put on various biofuel production routes and co-electrolysis. A large number of biofuel and e-fuel process models and data already exist. What is lacking now is a systematical way to evaluate and integrate them into an overall picture of the European transportation system.

In addition, the linear programming tool is quite mature in the lab and there are no special requirements for the mathematical knowledge. The main programming language of this project will be R or Python, depending on the background of the candidate.

The project will be organized in the following steps:

  • Conduct an exhaustive literature review and complete the concept structure in Figure 3.1.

  • Build database on the basis of existing models and data in the lab, including the following technologies:

    • Low carbon fuel manufacturing technologies

    • Corresponding powertrains for different low carbon fuels

    • Road transport vehicles

  • Develop the suitable sustainability metrics to compare and optimise the generated pathways from linear programming.

  • The decision support method based on multi-criteria analysis using the models to identify the most important options.
